Introduction¶

With the rise and availability of technology, younger generations have access to digital media. There have been many studies showing that social media can have a negative impact on mental health through cyberbullyig, comparing oneself to others online, and the addictive nature of social media. Now in a time where technology is needed to live, it is difficult to limit and protect users from these risks.

This project explores the dataset "Gen-Z Social Media Usage Dataset" available publicly on Kaggle. It contains 1,000,000 records each representing a unique Gen-Z social media user. The dataset includes behavioral, demographic, and psychological information on each user. The purpose of this project is to obtain insight on social media usage patterns and mental health trends among Gen-Z (ages 13-27).

Through this we could be able to identify who are most at risk, and causes of poor mental health early in youth.

Note: This is a synthetic dataset, but was designed to realistically resemble the modern digital world.

The screen_time_before_sleep column needed to be cleaned prior to creating the linear regression models. The purpose of all these models is to determine the factors that affect users' mental health, and specifically if social media has an impact. Comparing the simples linear regression models to the multiple linear regression model, we notice that daily usage hours is the primary driver of the model. It explain 58% of the variance in mental health scores on its own as we can tell by the r-squared of it's simple linear regression having the same value as the multiple linear regression adjusted r-squared.

num_platforms_used also showed to be significant on its own, but then had a high p-value when put with the other variables. This suggests that there is heavy multicollinearity with daily_usage_hours, which is expected because the more social media platform someone uses, the more they will spend time on social media.

screen_time_before_bed and avg_session_time proved to be insignificant on their own and in the multilinear model, suggesting they are not great predictors for mental health scores. I was surprised to find that scrren_time_before_bed was not correlated with mental health because typically losing sleep results in worsening mental health.

Conclusion¶

From this analysis, I found that this dataset supports the claim that social media has a negative impact on users. Specifically, those who spend excessive amounts of time or have multiple social media platforms are more vulnerable to poor mental health, regardless of age. The most probable solution using the findings pulled from the dataset is to limit usage of social media. With the biggest reason for using social media is entertainment, younger generations should pick up hobbies unrelated to technology as to not be reliant on one source to cure boredom.
